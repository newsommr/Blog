Blog Post 1 - 9/2/21

I watched a short but highly detailed video on YouTube titled HTML explained in 5 minutes. It detailed some of the history of HTML but mostly went through the basics that one would need to know before starting to code in HTML. For instance, it displayed how to use the bold and italic tags that I used in the last sentence. To make words be bold and italic, you use the "strong" and "em" tags, respectfully.

Another interesting thing that the video mentioned was the lists in html. The unordered lists seem particularly interesting to me because it can make tutorials much easier for someone else to read. Instead of just giving instructions in one line like this:

Step one: Visit google.com. Step 2: Go to privacy settings. Step 3: Turn off all personalized advertisements

You can use either the ordered or unordered list to make it much easier for the reader to follow along.
  • Go to google.com
  • Open privacy settings
  • Disable personalized advertising
